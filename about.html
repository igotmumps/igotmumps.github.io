About Me

Hi! I’m Michael Zacharias - Mumps Programmer. I work out of my home in Langley, British Columbia, Canada (I’m Canadian eh!). I contract out my skills as a Mumps programmer like a gun for hire! At any given time, there may be one or more of: two kids; two cats; or 1 wife in my office with me. So if I seem distracted, I am.

This blog, and its contents and opinions are strictly my own.

Growing up, I wouldn’t say that I was into computers much. I was interested in them, but laziness usually got the best of me. After high-school, I ultimately discovered the need for higher education and rekindled my passion for computer programming and technology. I graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from the University of Victoria. I first learned to program in Pascal, C/C++, Perl and later Java. I am not old enough to know Fortran or Cobol, but I have experienced Scheme/Lisp, and can remember DOS, Windows 3.1 and Visual Basic. After University, I got a job working with this strange thing called Mumps, and the rest is history.

I consider myself a fairly experienced Mumps programmer. I’ve been at it for over a decade working mostly in the banking vertical but also with medical and lab systems. Most of my work has been with the FIS Greystone Mumps flavor of Mumps but I have played around with Cache too. I am pretty passionate about this technology and love working with it!

Why am I blogging?

I heard it said once that the best way to really learn something is to teach it. That’s the main reason for starting this blog. With it, I hope to teach anyone reading it more about Mumps, and also to learn more about it myself. Plus it gives me a way to keep track of what I may be working on over time. My idea is to research how to do things in, with and about Mumps; then document them here. I fully anticipate this to be a two way conversation so email or comments are welcome. No spam though please!
